What is a run-on?

A run-on sentence happens when two or more complete ideas are joined together incorrectly. This does not mean long sentences are always wrong. A long sentence can work beautifully in a narrative essay when the reader can still follow the movement of the sentence.

A complete sentence usually has:

  • a subject
  • a verb
  • a complete idea

Fused sentence

A fused sentence pushes two complete ideas together with no punctuation.

I was the first person in my family to graduate from high school I knew college would be a challenge.

Comma splice

A comma splice joins two complete ideas with only a comma.

I didn’t know how to navigate financial aid, I taught myself by watching videos and asking questions.

Keep your voice

Narrative essays sometimes use longer sentences on purpose. For this activity, your goal is not to make every sentence short. Your goal is to make sure your reader can clearly follow each moment in your story.

Four ways to fix a run-on

There is not only one correct answer. The best fix depends on what you want the sentence to mean and how you want the moment to sound.

Use a period

Use this when both ideas deserve their own space.

During my senior year, I balanced school, sports, and a part-time job. It was exhausting.

Use comma + FANBOYS

Use this when you want to show how the ideas are connected.

  • for
  • and
  • nor
  • but
  • or
  • yet
  • so
During my senior year, I balanced school, sports, and a part-time job, and it was exhausting.

Make one part dependent

Use this when one idea depends on the other.

Words like because, although, while, and when can make part of a sentence dependent. That means it cannot stand alone anymore. It needs the rest of the sentence to feel complete.

Because I didn’t know how to navigate financial aid, I taught myself by watching videos and asking questions.

In this example, Because I didn’t know how to navigate financial aid is not a full sentence by itself. It sets up the reason, and the second part finishes the thought.

Use a semicolon

Use this when the ideas are closely connected and need a stronger pause than a comma.

I didn’t know how to navigate financial aid; I taught myself by watching videos and asking questions.

Choose the best fix for meaning

Sometimes more than one answer is grammatically correct, but one version does a better job showing the relationship between ideas.
